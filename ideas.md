# Design Ideas: The Job-Hopping Salary Calculator

## Approach A — "Financial Terminal" (Dark, Data-Dense)
<response>
<text>
**Design Movement:** Bloomberg Terminal meets modern fintech (dark data dashboard)
**Core Principles:** Data authority, numerical precision, professional gravitas, high contrast
**Color Philosophy:** Near-black background (#0a0a0a), electric green (#00ff88) for gains, amber (#f59e0b) for warnings, white for data — evokes financial trading terminals
**Layout Paradigm:** Split-screen: left panel inputs, right panel live chart updating in real time. No centered layouts. Monospace numbers.
**Signature Elements:** Animated counter numbers, glowing green "gain" figures, ticker-tape style scrolling stat bar at top
**Interaction Philosophy:** Every input change triggers instant animated recalculation — feels like a live trading dashboard
**Animation:** Numbers count up/down with easing, chart lines draw themselves, subtle scanline texture overlay
**Typography System:** Space Grotesk (display) + JetBrains Mono (numbers) — technical authority
</text>

## Approach B — "Brutalist Newspaper" (High-Contrast Editorial)
<response>
<text>
**Design Movement:** Swiss International Typographic Style meets editorial brutalism
**Core Principles:** Typography as architecture, stark contrast, no decorative elements, data speaks for itself
**Color Philosophy:** Pure white (#ffffff), pure black (#000000), one accent — deep red (#dc2626) for the "cost of loyalty" — no gradients, no shadows
**Layout Paradigm:** Newspaper grid — large headline numbers dominate, text columns below, asymmetric with bold rules and borders. The "gap" number is the hero.
**Signature Elements:** Oversized typographic numbers (the salary gap), thick horizontal rules as dividers, bold column headers
**Interaction Philosophy:** Slider inputs feel like adjusting a newspaper printing press — mechanical, satisfying
**Animation:** Minimal — only number transitions with a fast snap, no decorative motion
**Typography System:** Playfair Display (headlines) + IBM Plex Sans (body) — editorial gravitas
</text>

</response>

## Approach C — "Warm Wealth" (Premium Financial Advisory)
<response>
<text>
**Design Movement:** Luxury financial advisory meets modern SaaS — warm, trustworthy, premium
**Core Principles:** Trust through warmth, data visualisation as storytelling, generous whitespace, subtle depth
**Color Philosophy:** Warm off-white (#faf8f5) background, deep forest green (#1a4731) for growth/money, warm gold (#d4a853) for highlights, charcoal (#1c1c1e) for text — evokes wealth without coldness
**Layout Paradigm:** Vertical scroll with sticky calculator panel on right, narrative on left — like a premium financial report. Wide margins, generous padding.
**Signature Elements:** Smooth area chart showing diverging salary paths, animated "loyalty penalty" counter, subtle paper texture on cards
**Interaction Philosophy:** Sliders feel premium (custom styled), results animate smoothly — feels like a private wealth advisor's tool
**Animation:** Gentle fade-ins, chart paths draw with a smooth ease-out, number counters animate on change
**Typography System:** Fraunces (display serif for impact numbers) + DM Sans (body) — warm authority
</text>
<probability>0.09</probability>
</response>

## Selected Approach: C — "Warm Wealth"

The "Warm Wealth" approach is most appropriate for this content. It builds trust (essential for salary/financial content), feels premium without being cold, and the warm colour palette subconsciously connects to money and growth. The narrative-left / calculator-right layout mirrors how a financial advisor would present this information — authoritative but approachable. The Fraunces serif for impact numbers creates visual drama around the key statistics.
